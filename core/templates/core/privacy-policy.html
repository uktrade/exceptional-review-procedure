Privacy Policy

This notice sets out your rights with respect to how we will use your personal data. The Department for International Trade (DIT) recognises that it has a duty to people whose information it holds to treat that information responsibly, keep it safe and secure, and process it correctly and proportionately. This Privacy Notice broadly explains what information we collect, the purpose for processing, categories of personal information and who we may share it with.

Background

This service, “Feedback on the UK’s Temporary Tariff Regime” (also known as the Exceptional Review Procedure (ERP) is provided by the Department for International Trade (DIT) to allow UK businesses, individuals and importers from countries with preferential tariff rates to provide feedback on the impact of the UK’s temporary tariff regime.

Your Data

We collect personal data about you which covers details such as:

  • Full Name
  • Last Nafme
  • Organisation Name
  • Organisation or Personal email address (if a UK customer)
  • Number of employees in organisation

Purpose

The Department for International Trade (DIT) will collect, hold and process personal information about organisations for the purposes of assessing the impact of tariffs on certain sectors/industries and determining the tariff policy.

We may use the information you provide to us for consultation purposes, in order to allow DIT and other government departments to effectively target and plan the provision of future services. As part of this consultation process, you may be contacted.

We will not use your personal information for any other purposes, apart from what has been specified. However, if we intend to use it for any other new purpose/s we will ask you first. For instance, in some cases, DIT may wish to use your information for improving and developing our services. We will notify you of any significant changes or where necessary contact you for the purposes of obtaining consent.

The legal bases used to process your personal information is the performance of a public task (i.e. a task in the public interest or an official function which, in either case has a clear basis in law). It is necessary for DIT to perform this official function which has a clear basis in law.

How we may share your information

We will share information with other government departments including but not limited to HM Treasury, Department for Environment Food Rural Affairs, Business Energy and Industrial Strategy and Department for Transport to enable the review of the submissions, the development of policy recommendations and the assessment of the tariff policy’s impact on sectors/ industries. We will use Companies House lookup to find UK company details using the company name provided.

Please be aware that, in accordance with the Freedom of Information Act 2000, public bodies are required to comply with a statutory code of practice which includes obligations of confidence. In view of this, if you wish for your information to be treated confidentially, it would be helpful if you could explain to us the reason. If we receive a Freedom of Information request for disclosure of that information, we will take full account of your explanation, but we cannot give an assurance that confidentiality can be maintained in all circumstances.

How long we keep your information

We will retain your personal information for up to three years to fulfil the purposes we collected it for, including the purposes of satisfying any legal, accounting, or reporting requirements beyond the ERP period.

Your rights and access to your information

You have the right to request a copy of the information that we hold about you. The UK Data Protection Act 2018 also gives you additional rights that refer to how DIT holds and uses your information. Consequently, under certain circumstances, by law you have the right to:

  • withdraw consent and the right to object and restrict further processing of your data; however, where legally enforceable this may affect the service, we are able to provide to you. This applies to processing where the legal basis for processing your personal information is consent.
  • request to have your data deleted where there is no compelling reason for its continued processing and provided there are no legitimate grounds for retaining it.
  • request your data to be rectified if it is inaccurate or incomplete
  • not be subject to automated decision-making including profiling
